Animal Behavior

How would a deer get food?

1. Browsing They primarily browse, which means they eat leaves, twigs, and buds from shrubs and trees. This is especially common in fall and winter when grasses and herbs are scarce.

2. Grazing In spring and summer, they graze more often, feeding on: Grasses Wildflowers Clover Agricultural crops (like corn, soybeans, and alfalfa)

3. Seasonal Diet Shifts Their diet changes with the seasons: Spring/Summer: Green plants, fruits (like berries), and crops Fall: Acorns, nuts, and fallen fruit Winter: Woody plants, bark, and evergreen leaves

4. Feeding Behavior They usually feed during dawn and dusk (crepuscular behavior). They use their keen sense of smell to locate food. Their split upper lip helps them pick specific parts of plants.

How would a deer protect itself from predtors!?!?

1. Keen Senses Hearing: Large ears rotate to detect sounds from different directions. Smell: Excellent sense of smell helps detect predators from far away. Vision: Wide field of view and good night vision help them spot danger.

2. Camouflage Their brownish-gray coat blends into forests and fields, especially in fall and winter. Fawns have white spots that mimic dappled sunlight on the forest floor, helping them stay hidden.

3. Flight Response When threatened, they flee quickly, often running in a zigzag pattern to confuse predators. They can run up to 30 mph and leap over 8 feet high or 30 feet long in a single bound.

4. White Tail Signal When fleeing, they raise their white tail like a flag to warn other deer of danger.

5. Staying Still Sometimes, especially fawns, will freeze and stay motionless to avoid detection.

6. Group Behavior In groups, deer take turns being alert while others feed. More eyes and ears mean better chances of spotting danger early.
